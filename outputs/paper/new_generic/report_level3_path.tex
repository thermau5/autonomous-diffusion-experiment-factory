\begin{abstract}
The generic certificate of the companion report bounds excess terminal
risk by an irreducible floor $Q_0$ plus statistical and discretization
terms, with the probability path $P$ entering through \emph{both} the
floor $Q_0$ and the local solver defect $d$ (hence the optimal sampling
density $m^\star\propto d^{1/(p+1)}$). Every locked result so far varied
the schedule ($m$) and the solver core ($s$) at a \emph{fixed} EDM path.
Here we test the one axis those experiments held constant --- the path
$P$ itself --- using the Rectified-Flow reflow series (1-RF, 2-RF, 3-RF)
as a \emph{controlled path-straightening} ladder: identical NCSN++
backbone, identical $v$-parameterization, identical Euler solver, varying
\emph{only} the straightness of the probability path. The certificate
makes a sharp, falsifiable prediction: reflow lowers the curvature-driven
defect $d$ (helping at small NFE) but raises the floor $Q_0$ via coupling
approximation error (hurting at large NFE), so the reflow ladder must
exhibit a \emph{crossover} in the FID-vs-NFE plane rather than a uniform
win. We report the fixed-EDM frontier (Table~\ref{tab:edm}) and the path
ladder (Table~\ref{tab:path}) under the locked 10k Clean-FID protocol, and
we state plainly what the data confirm and refute.
\end{abstract}

\section{What this experiment isolates}
The hierarchy of the certificate is $h\!\to\!a$, $P\!\to\!(a,d,Q_0)$,
$s\!\to\!d$, $\R\!\to\!(a,d)$. The locked benchmark to date is a study of
the \emph{schedule} axis $m$ (and, with DPM-Solver-v3, the \emph{solver}
axis $s$) at a single, fixed EDM variance-exploding path. The path $P$ was
never varied. This report adds that axis with the cleanest controlled
manipulation available: the reflow procedure, which re-couples noise and
data along progressively straighter trajectories while keeping the network
architecture, the velocity parameterization, and the integrator fixed.

\paragraph{The certificate's path-axis prediction.}
Reflow reduces trajectory curvature, i.e.\ the local defect
$d_s(r)=A_\R(r)\,\tau_s(r)$ shrinks because $\tau_s$ (a curvature-weighted
truncation defect for a fixed-order solver) shrinks for straighter paths.
By $m^\star\propto d^{1/(p+1)}$ and the certificate's discretization term
$c_{\mathrm{disc}}\!\int d/m^p$, a smaller $d$ lowers the
\emph{NFE-dependent} part of the risk: straighter paths should win at
\emph{small} NFE. But each reflow step is trained to reproduce the
previous flow's input--output coupling, injecting an approximation error
that raises the \emph{floor} $Q_0$ (the published infinite-step FIDs grow
$2.58\!\to\!3.36\!\to\!3.96$ for 1/2/3-RF). The certificate therefore
predicts a \textbf{crossover}: 3-RF $<$ 2-RF $<$ 1-RF at low NFE, and the
order reversing as NFE $\to\infty$. A uniform win for any single member
would \emph{refute} the floor-vs-defect decomposition on the path axis.

\paragraph{Decision rule (pre-registered).} The crossover prediction is
\emph{confirmed} iff (i) at NFE$=5$ the FID order is 3-RF $<$ 2-RF $<$
1-RF (straighter wins when steps are scarce), and (ii) at NFE$=64$ the
order is 1-RF $<$ 2-RF $<$ 3-RF (the floor dominates when steps are
plentiful). Any monotone-in-NFE single winner refutes the
floor-vs-defect split on the path axis. We commit to this rule before the
2-RF/3-RF numbers are measured.
